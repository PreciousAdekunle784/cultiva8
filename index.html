The Crisis

African farmers grow the food and regenerate the soil but capture almost none of the value.

₦4.3 Trillion

Lost to post-harvest waste annually in Nigeria. That's 40% of harvested food destroyed by heat, moisture, and pests before reaching a single market.

<2%

Of global carbon credit revenue reaches African farmers, despite the continent hosting 60% of the world's uncultivated arable land and enormous sequestration potential.

Invisible Spoilage

Farmers have zero visibility into when their stored produce will degrade. By the time they notice, it's too late, 30-40% is already gone.

Locked Out of Carbon Markets

MRV costs $30-50 per soil sample. Smallholders can't afford the entry ticket to carbon markets.

No Buyer When You Need One

When produce is about to spoil, there's no system to find a buyer fast. Farmers sell at rock-bottom prices or lose everything.

Post-Harvest Intelligence

Know Exactly When Your Produce Will Spoil

Cultiv8 pulls real-time weather data from the Open-Meteo API: temperature, humidity, rainfall forecasts and runs it through FAO-calibrated spoilage models for every major Nigerian crop.

The result: a precise countdown showing how many days you have. When time runs short, we automatically alert nearby buyers, aggregators, and cold storage facilities. Selling at 70% price beats 100% loss.
