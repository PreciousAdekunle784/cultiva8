3. Platform Services

Cultiv8 provides the following services:

  • Carbon Sequestration Estimation: Using satellite imagery (Sentinel-2), soil data (ISRIC SoilGrids), and IPCC Tier 1 emission factors to estimate the carbon sequestration potential of registered farms based on reported regenerative practices.
  • Carbon Credit Marketplace: A platform connecting farmers and cooperatives with corporate buyers seeking verified carbon offset credits.
  • Post-Harvest Spoilage Prediction: Using real-time weather data (Open-Meteo API) and FAO-calibrated crop spoilage models to predict shelf life for stored agricultural produce.
  • Buyer Matching: Connecting farmers with nearby buyers, aggregators, processors, and cold storage facilities when produce is at risk of spoilage.
  • Weather Intelligence: Providing hyperlocal weather forecasts relevant to storage conditions and agricultural planning.

All services are provided on an "as-is" basis. While we strive for accuracy, the Platform's estimates, predictions, and recommendations are based on models and publicly available data and should not be treated as guarantees.

4. Carbon Credit Tracking & Marketplace

4.1 Carbon Estimation Methodology

Cultiv8 estimates carbon sequestration using IPCC Tier 1 default emission factors combined with satellite-derived NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) analysis. These estimates are approximations and may differ from values obtained through physical soil sampling or higher-tier methodologies.

4.2 Verification Standards

Cultiv8 aims to align carbon credit verification with internationally recognized standards including Verra VCS (Verified Carbon Standard) and Gold Standard. However, until credits receive formal third-party verification, they are classified as "estimated credits" and may not carry the same market value as fully certified offsets.

4.3 Marketplace Transactions

When you list carbon credits on the Cultiv8 Marketplace, you represent that the underlying regenerative practices have been truthfully reported. Cultiv8 facilitates the connection between sellers and buyers but does not guarantee the completion of any transaction. Pricing displayed on the Platform reflects market estimates and may fluctuate.

4.4 Revenue Sharing

Cultiv8 charges a service fee of 15-20% on completed carbon credit transactions. The remaining revenue is disbursed to the farmer or cooperative via mobile money (e.g., OPay, Paystack, Flutterwave) or bank transfer, subject to applicable transaction fees from payment providers.

5. Post-Harvest Intelligence Services

5.1 Spoilage Predictions

Spoilage predictions are generated using FAO-published post-harvest loss data combined with real-time weather information from the Open-Meteo API. Predictions are estimates based on crop type, storage method, temperature, and humidity. Actual spoilage may occur faster or slower than predicted due to factors not captured by the model, including pest infestation, physical damage, or pre-existing contamination.

5.2 Buyer Matching

Cultiv8 connects farmers with registered buyers based on crop type, location, and urgency. We do not guarantee that a buyer will be available, that a transaction will be completed, or that any specific price will be offered. All transactions between farmers and buyers are conducted directly between those parties.

5.3 Weather Data

Weather forecasts are sourced from the Open-Meteo API and NASA POWER, which aggregate data from multiple meteorological services. While generally reliable, forecasts may contain inaccuracies, particularly for hyperlocal conditions. Cultiv8 is not liable for decisions made based on weather forecasts provided through the Platform.
